Where evidence crosses over.

Umbral is an independent, open-source data lab. We build data products, causal analysis, and monitoring tools for the public interest — everything reproducible, everything documented.

Every published figure can be rebuilt from its source

Every dataset we publish ships with full provenance: origin URL, download date, license, and known caveats. Every number traces to a scripted transform — never a manual edit. If a figure cannot be reproduced, it is not published.

Methodology

Honesty and interpretability standards

  1. 01
    The title is the claim

    Every chart is titled with its finding, and the finding must be defensible from the data shown. No headline the evidence does not support.

  2. 02
    Deliberate causal language

    "Associated with" and "correlates" for descriptive work. "Effect" and "reduces" only with an explicit identification strategy, its key assumption stated next to the estimate.

  3. 03
    Uncertainty is always visible

    Intervals on projections, confidence intervals on estimates, n and denominator on rates. A point estimate without an interval is incomplete.

  4. 04
    Honest axes, visible gaps

    The y-axis starts at zero for bars; any truncation is annotated. Missing or underreported data is disclosed — never silently dropped or imputed.

  5. 05
    Human dignity

    Sensitive topics are handled with dignity: people are counted, never turned into spectacle.
